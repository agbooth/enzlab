How the program works

Enzyme selection

First, you must select the type of enzyme you wish to work with, using the Enzyme Selection dialog box. The characteristics of your enzyme are generated randomly, and the reference number of your enzyme displayed, along with some additional information which you should record. You can redisplay this information at any time by choosing Information from the Enzyme menu.

Starting the day

The program then simulates the course of a normal working day. When you arrive in the morning, you must decide how many samples of enzyme (if any) you wish to thaw for the day's work, and plan your experimental strategy for the day (see Planning your experimental strategy).

Experiments

During the day, the time is displayed and advances whenever you:

  • perform an experiment (see Performing an experiment)
  • analyse the precision of your experiments (see Assessing experimental precision)
  • analyse the enzyme's kinetics (see Performing a kinetic analysis).
  • obtain a pH profile of your enzyme (see Obtaining a pH Profile)

Ending the day

You can end a working day in several ways:

  • by choosing Go Home from the Go Home menu
  • by saying you don't want to work late when the computer reminds you of how late it is
  • if you are still working at midnight, the program sends you home anyway

At the end of each day, any remaining thawed enzyme is lost, and the program will ask if you wish to do another day's work. If you answer "yes", the next day starts; answering "no" ends the program.

Saving and loading

You can save all the work you have done to disk at any time, so that you can leave the program and come back to it later (see Saving your current work and Reloading your work from disk).
