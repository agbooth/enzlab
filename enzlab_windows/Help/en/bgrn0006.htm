Your experimental record

You must, as in real life, keep a record of your experiments as you perform them.

When you have selected an enzyme, you should record its name and reference number, and also its concentration and specific activity. (To redisplay this information at any time, choose Information from the Enzyme menu.)

Each 'day', you should write down what you propose to study. At the start of each series of experimental runs, make a note of what you are trying to find out and how you intend to do it.

Keep a sensible, tabular record of all your results, and plot each value of Vo on a suitable graph when you obtain it. (You can display the program's list of results by choosing List Results from the Results menu.)

When you have finished work with an enzyme, summarise your conclusions about its activity.
